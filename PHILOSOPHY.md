# Small Hours Games — Design Philosophy

## Core Vision

**Small Hours Games** creates interactive, creative, and competitive party game experiences designed for families and groups of all ages to play together on their home TV.

---

## Core Values

### 1. **Inclusive & Family-Friendly**

- Games are designed for mixed-age groups (kids, teens, adults, grandparents)
- No prerequisites: players pick up games instantly
- Zero skill barriers to entry — everyone can participate meaningfully from round one
- Vocabulary and mechanics avoid exclusionary jargon
- Content is age-appropriate and family-safe

### 2. **Social & Connected**

- Games are **fundamentally multiplayer** — single-player modes are bonus features
- Success in Small Hours comes from shared experience, not individual dominance
- Gameplay mechanics encourage banter, laughter, and group bonding
- Turn-based or simultaneous systems allow all players to feel engaged every moment
- Victory conditions celebrate group fun, not just winners

### 3. **Interactive & Engaging**

- Every player has meaningful input each round — no spectating
- Phones keep individual players engaged between their turns
- TV display shows what's happening for group awareness and shared anticipation
- Feedback (sounds, animations, scores) is immediate and celebratory
- No dead time; games maintain pace and momentum

### 4. **Creative & Expressive**

- Games invite players to express themselves (guessing, creating, strategizing, bluffing)
- Humor and creativity are rewarded as much as speed or knowledge
- Randomness and chaos are features, not bugs — unpredictability keeps everyone engaged
- Each round feels fresh because player decisions shape outcomes

### 5. **Accessible Technology**

- Works on any modern web browser (phones, tablets, laptops)
- No app installation required
- Low bandwidth requirements (works on weak WiFi)
- QR code to join instantly — no codes to type
- Graceful reconnection if someone's phone dies mid-game

---

## Design Principles

### Display-Centric Architecture

**The TV is the hub.** Small Hours games are built around a shared big-screen display that everyone watches together:

- **TV Display** shows the game state in full: current question, leaderboards, countdown timers, category themes, visual cards
- **Player Phones** are personal interaction devices — they show only what's relevant to that player (answer choices, input forms, prompts)
- No spoilers: players never see the answers on their own phones
- Shared anticipation: everyone watches the TV together, creating collective emotional moments

**Why this matters:**
- Keeps the group together around one focal point (the TV)
- Creates shared storytelling moments ("Did you see that response?")
- Prevents phone distraction — players focus on the game, not scrolling
- Enables dramatic reveals and reactions

### Synchronous & Fair Play

- All players act simultaneously or in clear turn order
- No hidden information that ruins surprises
- Game state broadcasts to all players at the same moment
- Scoring is transparent and visible to everyone
- Luck and randomness are part of the design (not flaws)

### Fast-Paced & Snappy

- Rounds complete in minutes, not hours
- Games are designed for 30–60 minute sessions
- Setup is zero: create room, share QR code, play
- No rule-heavy tutorials needed
- Feedback (points, reactions, leaderboards) appears instantly

### Replayability Through Variety

- Each round generates new content (questions, prompts, cards)
- Player creativity ensures no two games feel identical
- Multiple game modes prevent fatigue
- Quick rounds mean multiple games in one gathering

---

## Who Plays Small Hours

**Primary:** Families at home, friend groups gathering for game nights, house parties, casual meetups

**Secondary:** Bars and lounges (competitive, grown-up versions), classroom icebreakers, corporate team-building

**Not for:** Hardcore competitive gamers seeking deep strategy; asynchronous play; solo experiences

---

## What Small Hours Games Are NOT

- **Not competitive arena games** — PvP isn't the core, fun together is
- **Not abstract strategy** — Accessibility means minimal rules and quick comprehension
- **Not narrative-heavy** — Story serves gameplay, not the reverse
- **Not mobile-first** — Phones are controllers; the TV is the experience
- **Not always serious** — Chaos, randomness, and laughter are features
- **Not for serious eSports** — No grinding, no skill-gating, no ranking ladders

## Game Design Checklist

When designing or evaluating a Small Hours game, ask:

**Inclusivity & Accessibility:**
- Can a 6-year-old and a 60-year-old both play meaningfully? ✓
- Are rules learned in under 30 seconds? ✓
- Does the game avoid jargon and assumptions? ✓

**Social & Engagement:**
- Do all players contribute every round? ✓
- Is there space for banter and reactions? ✓
- Does the TV display drive shared emotion? ✓

**Interactive & Fair:**
- Can no player dominate unfairly? ✓
- Is randomness intentional, not broken? ✓
- Do scores feel earned, not arbitrary? ✓

**Technical:**
- Does the game work on weak WiFi? ✓
- Can players rejoin if disconnected? ✓
- Is setup truly zero-config? ✓

**Pacing:**
- Does a round complete in 2–5 minutes? ✓
- Does the game end before fatigue sets in? ✓
- Is feedback immediate (sounds, animations, scores)? ✓

## Examples in Practice

### Quiz
- **Inclusive:** Trivia questions span difficulty; power-ups let any player catch up
- **Social:** Reactions to surprise answers; shared leaderboard; group anticipation for reveals
- **Interactive:** Every player answers every question simultaneously
- **TV-centric:** Big display shows question, countdown timer, answer reveal, running scores

### Spy
- **Inclusive:** No reading required; game is pure guessing and discussion
- **Social:** Accusation and bluffing creates hilarious group dynamics
- **Interactive:** Everyone participates every round in voting
- **TV-centric:** TV shows the spy word, hints, and accusation count

### Shithead
- **Inclusive:** Simple card rules; any age can play
- **Social:** Trash-talking, strategic card plays, memorable comebacks
- **Interactive:** Simultaneous play; everyone's engaged
- **TV-centric:** TV shows deck, hints for what to play, score progression

## Long-Term Vision

Small Hours Games aspires to be:

1. **The default party game collection** for families and friend groups
2. **Accessible on any device** — TVs, laptops, tablets, phones (no app required)
3. **A living library** where new games are added regularly
4. **Open to creativity** — games that celebrate player expression and imagination
5. **Competitive without being toxic** — fun first, winning second

The ideal Small Hours session:
- Setup: 30 seconds (scan QR code)
- Play: 30–60 minutes of continuous laughter and engagement
- Teardown: None (just close the browser)
- Outcome: Everyone leaves wanting to play again

---

## Core Metrics

**Success** in Small Hours is measured by:

- ✓ First-time players understand within 60 seconds
- ✓ Groups ask "Can we play again?" after each game
- ✓ Kids and adults compete on equal footing
- ✓ Games create memorable moments worth retelling
- ✓ Zero frustration; maximum laughter

**Failure** looks like:

- ✗ Rules that require explanation or rulebooks
- ✗ One player dominating while others feel left out
- ✗ Frustration over unfairness or confusing mechanics
- ✗ Dead time or boring waiting phases
- ✗ Technical friction (reconnection, complex setup)

---

## Contributing with Philosophy in Mind

When adding features, games, or improvements:

1. **Ask first:** Does this serve the core values?
2. **Test with families:** Not just hardcore gamers
3. **Simplify ruthlessly:** Every rule should earn its place
4. **Celebrate chaos:** Randomness and unexpected moments are features
5. **Optimize for TV:** The big display is the main event
6. **Zero friction:** Setup, reconnect, join — all instant

If a feature makes the game less accessible, less fun in groups, or more complex, it doesn't belong in Small Hours.

## The Bottom Line

Small Hours Games exist so families and friends gather around a TV, phones in hand, and forget about the outside world for an hour. The games are vehicles for connection, laughter, and friendly competition.

Everything we build serves that moment.
